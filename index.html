Overview

Problem Description

Procrastination is a common problems that can have negative effects once out of hand. The constant struggle to meet deadlines, and lack of time to finish tasks can cause be overwhelming at times.
We wanted to design an application that could help the public alleviate this problem, and develop time management skills, in an entertaining way.
Proposal

User Research

In our research phase, we initially began by interviewing multiple potential users for their opinions on procrastination, and their own methods with dealing with it. For consistency we developed a script that would be read to each interviewee, which consisted of both warm-up questions and questions that focused on extracting specific information on the subject of procrastination and its effects.

Users and Personas

Personas

In our next phase we conglomerated our interview notes together on Miro, an online whiteboard, and separated our finding into individual ideas and perspectives. With these grouping, were were able to create 3 user archetypes, which served to guide our construction of our preliminary wireframes for our application.

The link below provides a copy of our research and archetypes.
User Research and Personas

User Stories and Epics

From our personas we created user stories and epics. With these, we were able to hone in on what features our users wanted and needed. For each epic, we created a scenario in which a feature or action was being used by a user. This exercise brought to light the possible intentions of the user, and the logistics of such actions and features.
After we completed this, we began designing a wireframe for our system, using the Figma workspace. Every feature that was created is the direct result of the epics and stories that we created from our user research.

Prototyping

In the wireframe we constructed, we initially had our user's character profile a the initial screen they saw when fist logging in. We also decided that the best way to navigate through our application would be through a navigation bar. Through this, the user would be able to check on their calendar and agenda. In our wireframe we thought it would be beneficial to include the task creation page, to gauge whether the pacing and process was intuitive.

Evaluation Results

Upon a second round of interviews, we created a revised wireframe. Many of the comments and suggestions we were able to gather from our subjects focused on the lack of navigation buttons on some screens, and the lack of detail and/or clarification with viewing and adding tasks to the agenda. We therefore, strove to add clearer buttons and included fake inputs inputs into some of the fields to better give users an idea of what would be expected from them when using our features.
